Middle, or heart notes, come next and last longer, typically forming the core of the scent. Think jasmine or cinnamon.</p><p class="pm-align--left">Base notes stay the longest, giving depth and warmth, like sandalwood or vanilla. Each note gets its turn. At first, the top notes attract you. After ten minutes, the heart notes begin to glimmer. These heart notes tend to evoke the most feelings and linger the longest.</p><p class="pm-align--left">Finally, the base notes ground the fragrance, sometimes for hours. Woody or smoky undertones might creep in long after the initial whiff.</p><p class="pm-align--left">In a lot of our favorites, you’ll detect grapefruit and rose on the top, lavender or nutmeg in the heart, and patchouli or musk at the base.
