## History

<p>Calamansi, a hybrid citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia, has a shorter and more contemporary history in fine perfumery compared to classic notes like Bergamot or Lemon. Traditionally, its uses were primarily culinary and medicinal within Asian cultures. It was not until modern perfumery began seeking more exotic, nuanced, and photorealistic fresh notes that Calamansi, with its unique blend of lime and mandarin characteristics, gained traction.</p>

<p>In the 21st century, perfumers started incorporating Calamansi to provide a sophisticated, sparkling, and vibrant opening that moves beyond typical European citrus accords. Its tartness, sweetness, and subtle floral nuances make it valuable for contemporary compositions, especially those aiming to evoke tropical freshness or a bright, mouth-watering energy. It is increasingly featured in niche and designer fragrances to lend an invigorating and luminous lift.</p>

## Famous Perfumes

<p>While Calamansi is a relatively modern note in perfumery, its vibrant, exotic profile has secured its place in contemporary fresh and tropical compositions. It offers a unique alternative to traditional lime and mandarin, providing a sparkling tartness that is often used to modernize classic citrus structures. It is frequently employed in niche and designer fragrances seeking an authentic, uplifting, and slightly mouth-watering opening.</p>
<ul>
<li>One notable example of a fragrance featuring the Calamansi note is **CK One Summer (2020 Edition)**. Here, the note is used to enhance the feeling of icy, aquatic freshness, blending its zesty tartness with grapefruit and mandarin for a bright, invigorating top layer suitable for hot weather.</li>
<li>Another fragrance that highlights the Calamansi note is **Acqua di Parma Chinotto di Liguria**. In this composition, the Calamansi works alongside the bitter chinotto and mandarin to create a complex, sun-drenched Italian citrus cocktail that is both sophisticated and intensely refreshing, contributing to the fragrance's signature sparkling quality.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Calamansi note is valued for its versatility in bridging green, herbaceous accords with juicy, sweet facets. Its intense yet delicate aroma makes it a favorite for perfumers aiming to create a prolonged, natural-smelling citrus burst, distinguishing fragrances like **Gucci Bloom Acqua di Fiori** where it adds a shimmering, watery brightness to the green floral heart.</p>

## Extraction Method

Extraction Methods of Calamansi

\<p\>The primary method for extracting calamansi essential oil is cold expression, where the fresh peel of the fruit is mechanically pressed or rasped to release the aromatic oils. Historically, this was often performed manually using the sponge method, which involved pressing the rind against a sponge to collect the oil, or the ecuelle a piquer method, where a bowl lined with sharp needles was used to puncture the oil glands of the citrus fruit.\</p\>
\<p\>In modern commercial production, extraction is typically achieved through high-speed mechanical peelers and centrifugal separators that isolate the oil from the resulting emulsion. Recent advancements include the use of supercritical carbon dioxide (scCO2) extraction, which operates at lower temperatures to produce a cleaner, more intense aromatic profile that more accurately reflects the fresh fruit. Additionally, molecular distillation is employed to refine the oil and remove specific unwanted components while concentrating the characteristic tangy and floral notes of the calamansi.\</p\>

## Trivia

Calamansi is so small and tart that it is often frozen whole and used as a citrusy, decorative ice cube in cocktails and iced teas.

## Seasonality

In tropical climates, calamansi trees are highly prolific and capable of blooming and bearing fruit year-round, though the peak harvest season typically occurs between August and October. While the fruit is most abundant during these late summer and early autumn months, its evergreen nature and consistent flowering cycles ensure a steady supply of its bright, tangy aromatic profile throughout the year. In perfumery, this year-round availability translates into a versatile, refreshing note that is as suitable for invigorating summer scents as it is for providing a zesty, uplifting quality during the cooler months.
